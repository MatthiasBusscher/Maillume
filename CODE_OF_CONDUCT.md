# Maillume Community Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

Maillume welcomes people who want to make suspicious email easier to assess. We expect participation to be respectful, patient, constructive, and safe for people of every background and experience level.

Helpful participation includes:

- giving specific, actionable feedback;
- assuming good intent while addressing harmful impact;
- explaining unfamiliar technical or security concepts without belittling people;
- respecting privacy, copyright, and responsible-disclosure boundaries;
- accepting maintainer decisions and moving disagreements to the appropriate issue or discussion.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized attention, personal attacks, deliberate disruption, publishing another person's private information, or pressuring anyone to disclose private inbox data or credentials.

## Project Safety

Never post real private email content, raw `.eml` files, private inbox screenshots, credentials, personal headers, tracking links, or exploit details in public project spaces. Follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) for vulnerabilities and [SUPPORT.md](SUPPORT.md) for product questions.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit or remove contributions, comments, issues, or other participation that violates this code. They may temporarily or permanently restrict participation when needed to protect the community or project.

Report conduct concerns to [support@maillume.io](mailto:support@maillume.io). Security vulnerabilities must instead follow the private process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md). Reports will be handled as confidentially as reasonably possible. Maintainers will disclose conflicts of interest and, when possible, ask an uninvolved person to review a report concerning a maintainer.

## Scope

This code applies in Maillume repositories and community spaces and when someone officially represents the project elsewhere.
